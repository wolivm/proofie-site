The problem

UX writing is the last thing reviewed and the first thing users read.

Copy lives scattered across hundreds of frames. It drifts off-brand, breaks its own rules, and ships before anyone reads it end-to-end. Spell checkers don't understand that a button isn't an error message — and that "Sign in" and "Log in" in the same file is a real bug.

Inconsistent

"Sign in", "Log in", "Login" — the same action, worded three ways, scattered across a file no single person reads cover to cover.

Manual

A human proofreads every screen by hand, late in the process, with no memory of the rule they enforced two frames ago.

Off-brand

Your voice lives in a style guide nobody opens mid-design. By the time copy is checked against it, the screens are already built.

proofie reads the whole file at once, knows what each string is, and checks it against your guidelines — not generic grammar rules.

Architecture

One click in Figma fans out to a parallel audit pipeline.

The plugin posts your selected frames to a Starlette backend. There's no slow agent loop — the orchestrator fires four audits at once with asyncio.gather, retrieves your guidelines from a vector store, then runs a single rewrite pass.

Embeddings

Real voyage-3 embeddings (1024-dim) for ingestion and retrieval — so the RAG matches meaning, not keywords.

Hallucination guard Every finding's quoted before text is verified to exist in the input copy. Fabricated quotes are dropped before the response leaves the backend.
Source citations Each prompt gets a numbered evidence pool (your guideline chunks + built-in rules). Findings carry a source_id resolved to a verifiable excerpt.
Rate limiting SlowAPI caps usage at 30 audits/hour and 60 guideline mutations/hour per IP, with SSRF and prompt-injection checks at the door.
Capabilities

Four audits, grounded suggestions, and a fix you can apply without leaving the canvas.

Tone audit

Is your voice consistent and on-brand? proofie compares every string against the tone rules in your style guide — not a generic rulebook.

Accessibility

Reading level, jargon, inclusive language, and screen-reader friendliness — the things that make copy usable for everyone.

Consistency

Detects "Sign in" vs "Log in" vs "Login" across the entire file, plus glossary violations against your preferred terms — for free.

Localization-ready

Opt-in checks for idioms, string concatenation, and hardcoded plurals — the things that quietly break when your copy ships in German.

Grounded suggestions

Every finding cites the specific guideline excerpt it's based on — so you can verify the rewrite isn't fabricated. Trust, built in.

Apply fix in place

One click rewrites the text node directly in your Figma frame. Every change is logged in History with one-click undo.
