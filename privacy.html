proofie. UX writing audit plugin for Figma

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026

proofie is a Figma plugin that reviews the writing in your designs and suggests improvements. This policy explains exactly what data proofie handles, who processes it, where it is stored, and how to delete it. It is written in plain language and describes the real behavior of the plugin — nothing is left vague or overstated.

The short version. proofie has no accounts and no login. It does not collect your name, email, or any payment details. It sends only two things to its backend for analysis: the text of the layers you choose to audit, and any style-guideline documents you upload. There is no advertising, no analytics, and no tracking. Your data is never sold.

1. Who runs proofie

proofie is an independent, single-developer project built and operated by Willian Magalhaes as a capstone for the Andela Agentic AI Accelerator. It is a personal/student project, not a company product. For any privacy question, contact iamwillian.magalhaes@gmail.com.

2. What data proofie processes

proofie processes only the data you explicitly hand to it:

  • Selected layer text. When you choose one or more frames or text layers in Figma and run a check, proofie reads the text content of those layers (the characters in them, plus the layer name and basic context such as whether it looks like a button or a heading) and sends that text to its backend to be analyzed and rewritten.
  • Uploaded style guidelines and glossary terms. If you upload a style guide document or add terminology in the Guidelines or Terms tabs, the text of those documents and term lists is sent to the backend so it can be indexed and used as the reference the audit compares your copy against.

What proofie does not collect. No names, emails, usernames, or passwords. No payment or billing information. No Figma account credentials. No contact lists. No location data. No advertising identifiers. proofie does not read layers you did not select for a check, and it does not scan your whole Figma file in the background.

Local-only preferences

Your interface-language choice and light/dark theme are saved locally on your own machine through Figma's plugin storage (figma.clientStorage). These preferences stay on your device and are never sent to the backend or to any third party.

3. Why proofie processes this data

The only purpose is to provide the plugin's core feature: analyzing your selected UI copy for tone, clarity, accessibility, consistency, calls to action, and localization, and proposing rewrites. Uploaded guidelines are used solely to tailor that analysis to your own writing standards. proofie does not use your content to train any model, build a profile of you, or for any purpose other than returning the audit results you requested.

4. Where your data goes — third-party processors

proofie's plugin talks to a single backend service over an encrypted HTTPS connection. That backend is hosted on Hugging Face Spaces at https://uxwillian-proofie.hf.space. To do its job, the backend forwards your data to a small set of independent third-party AI and infrastructure providers. Each is its own data processor governed by its own privacy policy:

5. Data retention and deletion

  • Audited copy is processed per request, not stored by proofie. When you run a check, your selected text is sent to the providers above to produce results and then returned to you. proofie does not keep its own database, log, or archive of the copy you audit. (How long each provider may transiently retain a request on their side is governed by their own policies, linked above.)
  • Uploaded guidelines and glossary terms persist until you delete them. These are stored in the Pinecone vector database so they remain available across sessions as your reference set. They stay there until you remove them.

How to delete your data

  • Delete a guideline or term: open the plugin's Guidelines or Terms tab and remove the item there. This deletes the corresponding entries from the vector database.
  • Clear local preferences: uninstalling or removing the plugin clears its local theme and language settings on your device.
  • Stop all processing: simply stop using or uninstall the plugin. With no accounts to close, there is nothing further to do.
  • Need help? Email iamwillian.magalhaes@gmail.com and your uploaded guidelines and terms can be removed for you.

6. No sale of data, no ads, no tracking

proofie does not sell, rent, or trade your data. It does not use your content or behavior for advertising, and it serves no ads. The plugin contains no analytics, telemetry, or tracking code, and it sets no cookies. The backend is protected by a shared secret token so that, in normal use, only the proofie plugin can call it.

7. Security

All communication between the plugin and the backend uses HTTPS encryption. Because proofie is an independent project, no system can be guaranteed perfectly secure; the providers listed above maintain their own security programs for the data they handle. Avoid submitting copy you are not comfortable sending to those third-party AI services (see the Terms of Service).

8. Children's privacy

proofie is a professional design tool intended for adults. It is not directed to children, and it does not knowingly collect any data from children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). Because there are no accounts, proofie does not knowingly process children's personal information.

9. International data transfers

The backend and the third-party providers above may process data on servers located in countries other than your own, including the United States. By using proofie you understand that the text you submit may be transferred to and processed in those locations under the providers' respective terms and safeguards.
